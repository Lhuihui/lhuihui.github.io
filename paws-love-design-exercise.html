Field Trip (2.5 hours)
After having a big picture of pet adoption ecosystem, I think knowing what's going on at shelters is very important, so I decided to have a field trip in Texas Human Heroes.
After seeing so many cute pets waiting in the cage, I developed a deep empathy for them. Most of them are very lone, and longing for a companion, geniune care and freedom.
I had some interesting findings about how shelters were delivering information to potential adopters. The pet information was in the paper hanging on the animal cage. I've chatted with shelter staff. They told me most of the pet age, breeds information is based their guess.
They are also hanging a lof educational information about how to read animals, how they would categorize animals based on how easy they can be handled.

Define project goals
After understanding the pet adoption system, and hearing my friends' stories about how they used to make decisions and how that effected the outcome of their pet relationship. I feel like the key problem is there is not enough data about the pet for adopters to make profound decisions.
Based on the shelter staff, their animals are from all over the place. When they got the animals, they didn't know their histories. They can only rely on the staff/volunteers' personal experience with the animals to help adopters to match the right one for them. Unfortunately, volunteers come and go. Most of the animal information didn't get fully transferred.
So I think one big part of the solution should be providing an easy pet information contribution system to collect as much as data about the pet. Another big part of the solution is to provide adopters a cognitive personalized pet content platform for pet discovery, matching, recommendation and pet raising education.
